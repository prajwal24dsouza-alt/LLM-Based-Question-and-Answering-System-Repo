Retrieval-augmented generation, often called RAG, combines document retrieval with large language model generation.
In a typical RAG pipeline, documents are cleaned, split into chunks, embedded as vectors, and stored in a vector database.
When a user asks a question, the system preprocesses the query, retrieves semantically similar chunks, and sends the question plus context to an LLM.
This design improves factual grounding and helps the model answer based on the supplied documents instead of relying only on its parametric memory.
